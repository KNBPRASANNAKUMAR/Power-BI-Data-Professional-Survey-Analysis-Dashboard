📌  **Project Overview**

This Power BI Dashboard provides an end-to-end analysis of Data Professional Survey responses collected from professionals across multiple countries and domains.

The dashboard helps analyze salary trends, programming language preferences, work-life balance, career satisfaction, and the difficulty of entering the data industry. The objective is to transform raw survey responses into actionable workforce insights for aspiring professionals, recruiters, and organizations.

This report delivers a business-intelligence-driven overview of the modern data ecosystem using interactive Power BI visualizations and KPI storytelling.


 🎯  **Problem Statement**

The data industry is rapidly evolving, but understanding compensation trends, skill demand, and employee satisfaction remains difficult due to fragmented survey data.

This dashboard was built to solve the following business problems:

- Analyze salary distribution across different data roles
- Identify the most preferred programming languages among professionals
- Understand work-life balance and salary satisfaction
- Measure how difficult it is to break into the data industry
- Compare participation trends across countries and job titles
- Support career planning using data-driven insights


 🧠 **Business Objectives**

- Analyze average salary by job title
- Identify top countries participating in the survey
- Evaluate employee happiness with salary and work-life balance
- Track programming language preferences among data professionals
- Understand industry entry barriers for aspiring professionals
- Build an interactive dashboard for workforce analytics


🔍  **Key Insights**

- Data Analysts reported the highest average salary among surveyed roles.
- Python emerged as the most preferred programming language among professionals.
- Most survey participants originated from the United States and India.
- Work-life balance satisfaction scored significantly higher than salary satisfaction.
- A large percentage of respondents reported difficulty entering the data industry.
- Data Engineering and Data Science roles showed growing salary trends.
